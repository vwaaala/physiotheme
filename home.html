Physiotherapy: Myths vs. Facts

Let's clear up some common misconceptions about physiotherapy.

Myth: Physiotherapy is Always Painful

Many people avoid physiotherapy fearing it will hurt. They believe 'no pain, no gain' applies universally.

Fact: Focus is on Healing, Not Hurting

While some discomfort can occur, our goal is pain reduction. Treatments are tailored to your tolerance, focusing on gentle movement and gradual progression to promote healing comfortably.

Myth: I Need a Doctor's Referral

It's often assumed that you must see a doctor first to get referred for physiotherapy treatment.

Fact: Direct Access May Be Possible

In many regions, you can directly book an appointment with a physiotherapist (Direct Access). We can help verify if your insurance or location requires a referral.

Myth: Physiotherapy is Only for Injuries

Some believe physiotherapy is solely for recovering from sports injuries or accidents.

Fact: It Addresses a Wide Range of Issues

Physiotherapy helps manage chronic pain, improve mobility, recover post-surgery, address neurological conditions, prevent injuries, and enhance overall physical function.

Myth: I Can Just Use Online Videos

With so many exercise videos online, people think they can self-treat effectively without professional guidance.

Fact: Personalized Assessment is Key

A physiotherapist provides an accurate diagnosis, creates a tailored plan, ensures correct technique, monitors progress, and adjusts treatment – things generic videos can't offer.
